## 5.3 Third-Party Risk
 
### Vendor Assessment and Selection
 
| Concept | Description |
|---|---|
| Due Diligence | Research before doing business: investigate finances, legal history, personnel, background checks. Verify all claims. |
| Conflict of Interest | Identify relationships that could compromise judgment: competitor ties, family relationships with vendor personnel, gifts offered for contract signing. |
| Right-to-Audit Clause | Legal agreement in the contract giving you the right to audit the vendor's security posture at any time. Verify security before a breach occurs. |
| Vendor Monitoring | Ongoing relationship management. Regular financial checks, IT security reviews, news monitoring. Assign a dedicated relationship manager. |
| Questionnaires | Direct security questions to the vendor: disaster recovery plans, secure storage methods, due diligence processes. Updates vendor risk analysis continuously. |
| Rules of Engagement | Document defining scope, purpose, and schedule of testing or access. IP address ranges, emergency contacts, in-scope/out-scope systems. |
| Supply Chain Analysis | Evaluate the entire chain from supplier to customer. Identify coordination gaps, assess IT systems supporting the operation. |
| Bug Bounty Program | Financial rewards for independent researchers who discover and responsibly disclose vulnerabilities. Controlled release: researcher reports, vendor fixes, public announcement follows. |
 
### Agreement Types
 
| Agreement | Description |
|---|---|
| SLA | Service Level Agreement. Minimum service terms: uptime guarantees, response time commitments, penalties for breach. |
| MOU | Memorandum of Understanding. Non-binding. States common goals and confidentiality. Not a signed contract. |
| MOA | Memorandum of Agreement. Step above MOU. Conditional agreement on objectives. May contain legally enforceable promises. |
| MSA | Master Service Agreement. Broad legal framework for all transactions between parties. Individual work details filled in by SOWs. |
| SOW / WO | Statement of Work / Work Order. Specific deliverables, scope, location, schedule, acceptance criteria. Used with an MSA. |
| NDA | Non-Disclosure Agreement. Protects confidential information. Formal contract with signatures required. Unilateral (one-way) or bilateral (mutual). |
| BPA | Business Partner Agreement. Governs the partnership: ownership stakes, decision-making authority, financial arrangements, contingencies. |
 
```
Agreement hierarchy:
BPA: governs the business partnership itself
MSA: broad legal framework for all transactions
SOW: specific work under the MSA
SLA: performance standards within contracts
MOU/MOA: pre-contract agreements establishing intent
NDA: confidentiality throughout the entire relationship
